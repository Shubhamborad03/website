# Conversion Rules — What Gets Visitors to Act

Patterns that drive leads, calls, and bookings. Based on real builds and competitor research.

---

## Homepage Must-Haves

1. **Phone number visible above the fold** — not hidden in a menu or footer
2. **Lead capture form on homepage** — inline, 4 fields max (name, phone, service, suburb/email)
3. **CTA button in navbar** — always visible as user scrolls
4. **Single primary action** — one clear thing you want them to do (call, book, fill form)

## CTA Language That Works

- "Start a conversation" — low commitment, personal (investor/strategist sites)
- "Get Your Free Quote" — high intent, trade/service sites
- "Book a Call" — mid commitment, professional services
- "I'M READY TO SCALE" (Hormozi) — bold, high-energy, filtering

**Avoid:** "Submit", "Contact Us", "Learn More" (vague, no urgency)

## Trust Signals (In Order of Impact)

1. Specific revenue/deal numbers ($80M+, not "extensive experience")
2. Client/partner logos as images (not text)
3. Real Google review count + rating
4. Licence numbers (QBCC for QLD trades)
5. ABN in footer
6. Years in business
7. Team photos (real, not stock)
8. Before/after project photos
9. Insurance/certification badges
10. Media logos (if applicable)

## Filtering Sections

- "Who I work with" sections that define the ideal client AND explicitly state who it's NOT for
- This filters out time-wasters and makes serious prospects feel identified
- Example: "If you're starting from zero, I'm probably not the right fit."

## Form Design

- 4 fields max on homepage forms
- Name + Phone are mandatory
- Service type as dropdown (pre-qualifies)
- Never ask for address on first contact — too personal
- Submit button = specific action language, not "Submit"

## Mobile Conversion

- Sticky phone button on mobile (bottom of screen)
- Click-to-call on all phone numbers
- Form must work without scrolling horizontally
- CTA button must be thumb-reachable
